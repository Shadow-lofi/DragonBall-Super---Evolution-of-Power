Dragon Ball Super: Evolution of Power

Explore the differences between the manga and TV series, power levels, and much more!

Manga vs TV Series

Manga

  • Author: Toyotarou (supervised by Akira Toriyama)
  • Pacing: More concise storytelling with focused plot development
  • Art Style: Detailed illustrations with manga-specific techniques
  • Character Development: Streamlined character arcs
  • Power Scaling: More consistent and balanced
  • Tournament of Power: Shorter, more focused battles
  • Moro Arc: Exclusively in manga (not animated yet)
  • Granolah Arc: Current manga arc exploring new power levels

TV Series (Anime)

  • Studio: Toei Animation
  • Pacing: Extended episodes with more filler content
  • Art Style: Animated with varying quality across episodes
  • Character Development: More screen time for supporting characters
  • Power Scaling: Sometimes inconsistent due to episode padding
  • Tournament of Power: Extended battles spanning multiple episodes
  • Exclusive Content: Some anime-only scenes and transformations
  • Music: Original soundtrack enhancing emotional moments

Key Story Differences

Goku Black Arc

The manga version features different power-ups and battle sequences. Vegito's appearance is shorter in the manga, while the anime extends the fight significantly.

Tournament of Power

The anime version is dramatically longer, showcasing more fighters and eliminations. The manga focuses on key battles and strategic eliminations.

Ultra Instinct

The anime shows Goku's gradual mastery through multiple incomplete forms. The manga presents a more straightforward path to mastery.
